Myanmar Tradational Foods

Mohinga

Opened a decade ago, the favorite part of the meal is the soup. With a generous amount of meat and bones, it is kept warm in a clay pot. If grounded garlic and coriander leaves are added, the warm and savory soup would leave a mark on one’s taste buds. One can choose chicken or pork hmee shay, and it would go in well with a large amount of meat included, together with one’s choice of just the right amount of chili sauce, pai ngapi (bean fish paste), grounded garlic, bean sprouts, kaw yay (liquid starch) and ei kyar kway (fried breadstick). The shop is open until 11am, with a big serving costing K1000 and a small one K700. Though these shops in Mandalay do not have attractive furnishings, the taste of the food served is remarkable.

About This Project

The aim of this website is to promote interest in Myanmar's famous place and great pagoda.Our hope is to challenge the often two-dimensional and uncritical views of history taught in Myanmar by reexamining key periods in the country's past. We would like as well to reveal the rich multicultural nature of Myanmar's famous pagoda,food and its deep and ongoing ties to global events and trends.Finally, this website will also delve into the famous pagoda and myanamr tradational food of neighbouring regions, such as Yunnan in China and the Northeast in India, in the hope of introducing these places to a Myanmar audience.We believe that meeting Myanmar's many challenges today will require fresh imaginative thinking. This is turn will only be possible with a clear and critical understanding of Golden Places Of Myanmar.
